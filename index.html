A Deeper Dive Into Each Technology

Classic Bots

Positioned in the bottom-left, these are the most basic systems. They operate on fixed rules and decision trees. Their intelligence is low because they cannot understand context beyond their programming, and their autonomy is minimal as they only react to specific user commands.

Robotic Process Automation (RPA)

RPA is placed high on autonomy but low on intelligence. These bots are workhorses designed to automate repetitive, rule-based digital tasks (e.g., data entry). They can execute long sequences of tasks without human intervention (high autonomy) but lack the intelligence to adapt to new situations or understand unstructured data.

Conversational AI

Located in the middle, these systems (like advanced chatbots or voice assistants) use Natural Language Processing (NLP) to understand and respond to human language. This gives them a moderate level of interaction intelligence. Their autonomy is also moderate, as they can guide a conversation to a goal but typically don't act beyond that dialog.

Cognitive Automation

This represents the evolution of RPA, infused with AI capabilities. By incorporating technologies like computer vision or NLP, these systems can handle unstructured data (e.g., reading an invoice) and make simple decisions. This increases both their intelligence and their useful autonomy compared to traditional RPA.

Generative AI

Positioned with high interaction intelligence, these models excel at understanding complex prompts and generating novel, context-aware content (text, images, code). Their autonomy is limited because they are primarily reactive, requiring a human prompt to initiate creation. They don't typically act on their own to achieve a goal.

Multimodal AI

This is a key capability of advanced AI, often overlapping with Generative AI. These models can process and integrate information from multiple sources (e.g., text, images, and audio) simultaneously. This leads to a richer, more human-like interaction intelligence and enables more complex, autonomous behavior in agents that perceive the world through multiple senses.

Agentic AI

Representing the cutting edge, Agentic AI sits at the top-right. These are autonomous systems designed to achieve complex goals with minimal human oversight. An "agent" uses other AI models (often generative and multimodal) as its "brain" to perceive its environment, create a plan, and execute actions. They are the pinnacle of both interaction intelligence and autonomy.

Notes:
• Generative AI sits high on interaction capability but only gains autonomy when orchestrated (tools/agents).
• Agentic Systems area is limited to solutions with planning + tool use + execution.
• Cognitive Automation is above RPA due to perception/decision components.
